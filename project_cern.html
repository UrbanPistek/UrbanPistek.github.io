About the Team

CERN's beamline for students internal competition had teams of high school students competeting to design a particle physics experiment that can be tested on CERN's proton synchrtron. Our team (Team Investigate Tachyons) proposed an experiment that would test for exsistance of the mathematically derived tachyon particles outlined in our project proposal. We managed to get coverage on the news, ran a math club in highschool, met with local MLA's and Alberta's Minister of Education, developed a wesbite and created monthly newsletters covering various math and physics concepts. Finally, I had the opportunity to give a keynote talk at the Telus Spark Science Center explaining our project and the beauty of math and physics.
